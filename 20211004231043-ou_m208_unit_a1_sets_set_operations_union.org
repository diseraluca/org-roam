\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Set Union]
Let $A$ and $B$ be any two sets; then the \textbf{union} of $A$ and $B$ is the set

\begin{equation*}
A \cup B = \{x : x \in A\ \text{or}\ x \in B\}
\end{equation*}
\end{tcolorbox}

For example, if \(A = \{2,3,5\}\) and \(B = \{1,2,5,8\}\) then their union, the set of all elements belonging to at least one of \(A\) or \(B\), is \(\)\(\{1,2,3,5,8\}\).
