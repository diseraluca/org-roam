#+title: OU:M208:Unit-A1:Functions:Distance Function
#+filetags: OU M208 Mathematics Functions Distance Plane Reals Points

Functions of the form \(f : \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) can be used to specify quantities associated with points in the [[id:b9a24444-23bc-4ac9-b1b8-06ee664f15c5][OU:M208:Unit-A1:The Plane]].

For example:

\begin{align*}
f : &\mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\
    &(x,y) \mapsto \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}
\end{align*}

Calculates the distance between a point and the origin.
 * See [[id:ff987959-8bd5-45b1-8f73-09637c52f1a8][OU:M208:Unit-A1:Distance Between Two Points in the plane]] to understand how to calculate the distance between two points in the plane.
