+ Real Function :: A function whose domain and codomain are both subsets of \(\mathbb{R}\).

  In some texts the restriction on the domain of a real function is lifted, meaning that, while the codomain still has to be a subset of \(\mathbb{R}\), the domain may be any set.

For example:

\begin{align*}
f : &\mathbb{R}^* \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\
    &x \mapsto \frac{1}{x}
\end{align*}

And:

\begin{align*}
f : &\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\
    &x \mapsto 2x - 5
\end{align*}
