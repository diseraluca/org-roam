\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Two-line Notation]
Let $f$ be a symmetry of a polygon $F$ that has vertices at locations labelled $1,2,3, \ldots, n$.

The \textbf{two-line symbol} representing $f$ is:

\begin{pmatrix}
1&2&3&\ldots&n\\
f(1)&f(2)&f(3)&\ldots&f(n)
\end{pmatrix}

where $f(1), f(2), f(3), \ldots, f(n)$ are the labels of the locations to which $f$ moves the vertices originally aligned at the locations labelled $1,2,3,\ldots,n$, respectively.

We say that $f$ is written in \textbf{two-line notation}
\end{tcolorbox}
