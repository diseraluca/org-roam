Knowing tha the number \(x + iy\) corresponds to the point \((x,y)\) the plane, we can form an alternative description of complex numbers.

#+LATEX_HEADER: \usepackage{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[Polar Form]
A non-zero complex number $z = x + iy$ is in \textbf{polar form} if it is expressed as

\begin{equation*}
z = r(\cos{\theta} + i\sin{\theta})
\end{equation*}

where $r = \left|z\right|$ and $\theta$ is any angle between the positive direction of the real axis and the line joining $z$ to the origin.

Such an angle $\theta$ is called an \textbf{argument} of the complex number $z$, and is denoted by $\text{arg}\ z$.

The \textbf{principal argument} of $z$ is the value of $\text{arg}\  z$ that lies in the interval $(-\pi. \pi]$ and is denoted by $\text{Arg}\ z$.
\end{tcolorbox}
