#+title: OU:M208:Unit-A3:Mathematical Statements:The Negation of An Implication
#+filetags: OU M208 Mathematics Proofs Statements Implications Negations Conjunctions

Negating, see [[id:4b3532c9-547b-499f-940b-c43a492fbdbe][OU:M208:Unit-A3:Mathematical Statements:Statements And Negations]], an [[id:f756858e-424c-405c-969d-b83b35b665d5][OU:M208:Unit-A3:Mathematical Statements:Implications]] does not produce another implications.
Instead, it produces a conjunction, see[[id:e9ca943b-7537-4764-b218-b59db2ce82ce][ OU:M208:Unit-A3:Mathematical Statements:Conjunctions And Disjunctions]].

You can think of the implication "If P, then Q" as saying that "it is not the case that P is true and Q is false".

Trivially, the negation of this statement is "P and not Q".
