\begin{tcolorbox}
Let $P$ be any point in $\mathbb{R}^2$ or $\mathbb{R}^3$.
The \textbf{position vector} of $P$ is the vector whose starting point is the origin and whose finishing point is $P$, that is, the vector $\overrightarrow{OP}$, where $O$ is the origin.
\end{tcolorbox}

 * For example, the position vector of \(P(2, -1)\) is \(\overrightarrow{OP} = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j}\).

Let O be the origin, then:

\begin{align*}
\overrightarrow{AB} &= \overrightarrow{AO} + \overrightarrow{OB} \\
                    &= -\overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OB} \\
                    &= -\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} \\
                    &= \mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a} \\
\end{align*}

Thus, for any vector \(\overrightarrow{AB} = \mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}\).
